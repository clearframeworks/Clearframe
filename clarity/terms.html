Terms / scope

Clarity provides structured decision support and constraint analysis. Clarity is not emergency services, and it is not medical, legal, or financial advice.

If someone is in immediate danger or active survival crisis (no shelter, no food, safety risk, acute medical or mental health crisis), the correct move is direct services and local emergency resources first.

Clarity is appropriate when there is still meaningful agency: decisions can be made, steps can be taken, and stability can be improved over time.
